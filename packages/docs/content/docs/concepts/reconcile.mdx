---
title: Reconcile
description: When the branch moves under a pending draft, every comment is classified clean, drifted, or lost and decided explicitly.
---

A review comment anchors to a specific commit and a specific line. That commit can move: the
PR gets force-pushed, the base advances, the head changes between the moment you
[synced](/docs/concepts/snapshot) and the moment you submit. Most tools handle this by
failing — they reject the submit and, too often, lose the work. revu handles it with
**reconcile**: when you submit a [draft](/docs/concepts/drafts) against a head that has moved,
every pending comment is classified and you decide each one explicitly. Nothing is ever
silently discarded.

<img src="/assets/screenshots/reconcile.png" alt="The reconcile screen: the branch moved mid-review, and every pending comment gets an explicit decision" />

## The three classifications

When submit detects a moved head, it does not post anything. Instead each pending comment is
scored against the new head and lands in one of three buckets:

| Class | Meaning | The default decision |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Clean** | The line the comment anchors to is unchanged at the new head | Accept — the comment re-anchors cleanly and posts as written |
| **Drifted** | The anchored content still exists but has moved to a different line | Re-anchor to its new location, or drop it |
| **Lost** | The anchored content is gone at the new head — the lines were changed or deleted | Decide explicitly — keep it as an out-of-line note, or drop it |

"Drifted" is a staleness state, so it wears the same gold the [snapshot seal](/docs/concepts/snapshot)
uses for time that moved under you.

## An explicit decision per comment

Reconcile is a screen you walk, not a thing that happens to you. For each comment you
**accept**, **re-anchor**, or **drop** — deliberately. The draft survives the entire flow;
nothing is posted until you finish reconciling and resubmit, and nothing is removed from your
draft store without your having chosen to drop it. That is the guarantee: the branch can move
as many times as it likes, and your review is never quietly thrown away.

The scorer that classifies comments is the same pure re-anchor logic the submit path runs, so
what the reconcile screen previews is exactly what submitting does — the preview and the
action cannot diverge.

## Where it fits in submit

1. You submit a draft. revu guards first by checking the current head against the head you
   synced.
2. **Head matches** — the review posts, and the draft is deleted on confirmed success.
3. **Head moved** — nothing posts; you are routed into reconcile with every comment
   classified clean / drifted / lost.
4. You decide each comment, then resubmit against the fresh head. The draft is intact the
   whole time and is only deleted once the resubmit confirms.

Even a force-push in the narrow window *after* the guard passes but *before* the post
completes keeps the draft: the failed comment surfaces as a conflict and reconcile runs again.
