A pending comment is anchored to a specific commit. When the branch is
force-pushed between the moment you synced and the moment you submit, those
anchors point at code that may have moved or vanished. Most tools throw the
review away. revu routes you through **reconcile** instead: every pending comment
gets an explicit decision, and nothing is ever silently discarded.

Follow along in mock mode with fixture **#389** — a stale snapshot and a draft
against an old head, which triggers the full reconcile flow.

## When reconcile happens

You reach reconcile when you [submit](/docs/guides/submitting) a draft and revu's
head guard finds that the PR's head commit is no longer the one your snapshot was
taken against. Submit posts nothing, keeps your draft intact, and opens the
reconcile dialog. You can also re-sync (`⇧` `R`) a PR whose snapshot seal has
gone gold and reconcile from there.

## The three classifications

revu re-runs each pending comment's anchor against the new head and sorts it into
one of three buckets:

| Class | What it means | Default decision |
|---|---|---|
| **Clean** | The anchored lines are unchanged on the new head. | Keep — re-anchors automatically. |
| **Drifted** | The anchored lines moved but still exist. | Re-anchor to the new location, or drop. |
| **Lost** | The anchored lines are gone on the new head. | Drop, or move to a nearby line. |

![The Reconcile dialog: pending comments grouped into Anchors cleanly and Drifted, each with its file and line, over the underlying diff, with Cancel and Apply and submit review actions](/assets/screenshots/reconcile.png)

## Walk the list

1. Open reconcile (from a blocked submit, or after a re-sync of a stale
   snapshot).
2. Comments are grouped by class. **Clean** ones are already handled; review them
   and move on.
3. For each **drifted** or **lost** comment, make a decision explicitly: accept
   the re-anchor, move it to a nearby line, or drop it.
4. When every comment has a decision, **Apply and submit review** — reconcile
   re-anchors the kept comments and submits in one step. **Cancel** keeps the
   draft untouched so you can come back to it.

The classifier that previews these decisions is the exact same code that runs on
submit, so what the dialog shows is what actually gets posted. For the full
model, see [Reconcile](/docs/concepts/reconcile).

## Related situations the demo covers

Reconcile is one of several ways the world can move under a snapshot. The other
fixtures show the neighboring cases:

- **#410** — the base branch advanced but the head did not. The diff still
  changed (it is a three-dot compare), and the snapshot seal says so. See
  [`compareKey`](/docs/concepts/compare-key) for why base moves matter.
- **#415** — a thread was resolved elsewhere since you synced, with zero new
  commits. A re-sync reuses every blob and just refreshes the mutable half.
- **#401** — a sync died partway through, leaving a partial snapshot that names
  what is missing; a retry fetches only the missing pieces.
